Flagship Longitudinal Study

Rio Grande 2019 Birth Cohort: Following the Future Generation of Rio Grande

More than a study, this cohort is a living partnership with over 2,300 children and their families. Together, we are mapping how early life experiences shape health, learning, and wellbeing for decades to come.

2,300+ families A near-complete generation of babies born in Rio Grande in 2019.
99.5% coverage Inclusion of nearly every birth in the municipality that year.
Decades of follow-up From birth to adulthood, tracking growth, health, and resilience.
Why it matters

A living map of a generation's health and opportunity

By walking beside families from the first day of life, the cohort unlocks evidence that transforms health services, education, and community wellbeing across Rio Grande.

Understand child development: We document growth, neuropsychomotor milestones, and social skills to see what accelerates or challenges healthy development.

Inform public policy: Findings power actionable recommendations so health and education leaders can target real needs with precision.

Support maternal wellbeing: We surface protective factors that prevent mental health issues, helping mothers feel supported and confident.

Build community legacy: Every insight feeds a long-term evidence base that benefits today's families and the next generation.

Methodology

Scientific rigor, ethical care

Our approach blends gold-standard epidemiological methods with respectful, participant-centred practices.

Detailed data collection

Interviews at maternity wards and during home visits capture family context, health history, and developmental milestones. Standardised questionnaires are hosted on RedCap for accuracy.

Privacy and confidentiality

Data is encrypted, stored securely, and governed by strict ethical approvals so participants can trust every interaction.

Longitudinal follow-up

Visits at 6 and 30 months—and beyond—allow us to spot trends and interventions that short-term studies can't see.

Milestones in our follow-up journey

Explore the major touchpoints we've shared with families so far—and what we're preparing next.

Initial interviews in the maternity wards gather detailed maternal health histories, prenatal care journeys, and early bonding indicators within hours of birth.

Home visits assess breastfeeding, sleep patterns, and early stimulation activities, while screening for postpartum mental health support needs.

Comprehensive developmental evaluations measure language, motor skills, and socio-emotional wellbeing, paired with household environment insights.

Upcoming assessments focus on school readiness, early learning experiences, and the role of childcare and community programs in nurturing growth.

Plans for primary school and adolescent follow-ups will map long-term health, education, and social outcomes, continuing the cohort's legacy for decades.
