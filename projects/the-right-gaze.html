The Right Gaze: Unveiling Emotions in Mother-Child Interactions with Innovative Technology

Headcams and AI analytics bring us closer to the emotional choreography of parenting, capturing authentic moments that shape development and wellbeing.

Why it's different

Seeing family life through their own eyes

Traditional observation alters behaviour. The Right Gaze keeps interactions natural, combining human warmth with high-fidelity data.

Real perspective: Head-mounted cameras on both mother and child reveal what they see, without external observers.

Emotion decoding: AI tools like FaceReader analyse micro-expressions, vocalisations, and synchrony to capture joy, stress, and connection in milliseconds.

Objective insights: Behavioural patterns become measurable data, helping us identify supportive dynamics and early warning signs.

Innovation spotlight

Families record their own daily routines—mealtimes, play, bedtime—while an ambient camera frames the whole environment. AI stitches perspectives together for unparalleled clarity.

Methodology

Technology with empathy at its core

Rigorous protocols and ethical guardrails ensure every recording respects family privacy and agency.

Wearable tech

Lightweight headcams for mother and child capture first-person views, while a frame camera provides contextual footage.

AI interpretation

Machine learning models quantify facial expressions, vocal tone, and attention synchrony to reveal the rhythm of connection.

Privacy first

Encrypted storage, strict consent protocols, and participant control over recordings keep trust at the centre.

Expected impact

Transforming parenting support with objective evidence

The Right Gaze turns intimate moments into actionable insights for families, professionals, and policymakers.

Understand parenting styles

Map how different interaction patterns influence child development and maternal mental health.

Identify early signals

Spot stress markers and attachment challenges before they escalate.

Design better interventions

Tailor support programs using evidence grounded in lived experience.
