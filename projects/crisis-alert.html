Crisis Alert: Tracking the Mental Health Footprint of COVID-19 and the RS Floods

When crises hit, our cohort becomes an early-warning system. We documented how the pandemic and 2024 floods transformed daily life, stress, and resilience.

Why now

Capturing crises in real time

Our longitudinal design allowed us to pivot quickly, measuring emotional, social, and physical impacts exactly when families needed support.

COVID-19 adaptation

Online surveys monitored maternal depression, anxiety, and changes in routine, work, and caregiving during the pandemic's peak uncertainty.

Flood response (RS 2024)

A dedicated follow-up assessed mental health trajectories before and after the floods, comparing displaced families with those who sheltered in place.

Multi-layered analysis

Understanding who is most vulnerable

Our multidimensional framework blends geography, socioeconomics, and lived experience to prioritise support.

Geospatial risk

Map impact zones, displacement duration, and material losses across Rio Grande do Sul.

Social determinants

Layer income, employment, and support networks to see which families need urgent resources.

Mental health trajectories

Track how stress, fear, and resilience evolve over time, identifying protective factors.

Innovation in crisis

AI reveals the unseen impact

We combine qualitative empathy with quantitative precision to understand intergenerational stress.

Policy-ready dashboards: Communicate findings to civil defence teams planning future responses.
