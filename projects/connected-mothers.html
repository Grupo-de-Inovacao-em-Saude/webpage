Mothers Connected: Low-Cost Digital Support for Maternal and Child Health

WhatsApp becomes a lifeline for evidence-based advice, peer connection, and self-efficacy. Our randomised study measures how digital support transforms family wellbeing.

The vision

Accessible support that meets mothers where they are

In low-resource settings, specialised care is often out of reach. Connected Mothers turns everyday messaging into a personalised support network.

Structured messaging

Evidence-based content covers mental health, nutrition, sleep hygiene, and practical strategies for weekly goal setting.

Community connection

Peer groups share experiences and encouragement, moderated by trained health professionals to maintain safety and relevance.

Methodology

A randomised design with real-world reach

We blend clinical rigour with digital accessibility to understand what truly works for mothers and babies.

Participants join through the cohort network and community partners, ensuring inclusion of diverse maternal experiences.

Mothers are randomised into informative messaging, peer-support, or control groups to isolate the impact of each component.

Moderators guide weekly discussions, share multimedia resources, and encourage reflection through WhatsApp.

Validated instruments like EPDS, BISQ, and self-efficacy scales capture changes in mental health, child outcomes, and confidence.

Successful strategies are prepared for rollout with public health partners, adapting content to new communities.

Expected outcomes

Measuring impact across family wellbeing

We track how digital support influences health, behaviour, and confidence for mothers and their children.

Maternal mental health

Reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety while boosting self-efficacy in daily care routines.

Child nutrition & sleep

Track improvements in feeding practices, picky eating, and sleep quality through parent-reported outcomes.

Scalable intervention

Demonstrate how low-cost messaging can complement clinical care in low- and middle-income countries.
